The classic light switch design was patented by William J. Newton in 1906. His design for a simple, vertical toggle switch became the standard and remains largely unchanged in modern homes today.
Joysticks were originally developed for military flight simulators in the 1950s before becoming a popular input device for video games in the 1970s!
The original digital button is often credited to the "press button" on early telephones, invented by Almon Strowger in 1891. His design replaced the rotary dial with a button that could be pressed to dial numbers, marking a shift toward more intuitive, digital input methods in communication technology.
One of the most famous accidental prints occurred in 1997, when a British government department printed and distributed thousands of confidential documents due to a misclick on the print button.
Before graphical user interfaces (GUIs), early computer users interacted with machines by typing commands into terminals, controlling everything through text-based instructions.
